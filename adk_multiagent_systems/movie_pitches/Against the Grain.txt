Logline: In a world where a devastating crop disease threatens to plunge a nation into famine, a brilliant and tenacious plant pathologist defies societal expectations and scientific skepticism to unlock the secrets of the blight, ultimately saving Canada's vital wheat supply.

Synopsis:
Act I: The Blight and the Burden
*   Introduction to Margaret Newton: A driven and intelligent young woman, passionate about science, but living in an era where women in scientific fields face significant barriers and skepticism. She is an outsider, but fiercely determined.
*   The Crisis: Canada's wheat crops are being ravaged by stem rust (*Puccinia graminis*), threatening widespread famine and economic collapse. Farmers are desperate, and the government is struggling to find a solution.
*   Margaret's Calling: Witnessing the devastation firsthand, Margaret feels a profound responsibility to find a cure. She dedicates herself to studying the mysterious disease, often working in isolation and facing dismissive attitudes from male colleagues who doubt her capabilities. She secures a position, perhaps a junior one, that allows her to begin her research, but resources are scarce.

Act II: The Relentless Pursuit
*   The Scientific Battle: Margaret immerses herself in painstaking research, spending countless hours in the lab and fields. She faces numerous setbacks, failed experiments, and the physical toll of her relentless work, often pushing herself to exhaustion.
*   Breakthroughs and Resistance: Through her meticulous observation and innovative methodologies, Margaret begins to unravel the complex life cycle of *Puccinia graminis* and identify different strains of the rust. Her findings are revolutionary, but she encounters resistance from established scientific figures who are slow to accept her unconventional approaches or the implications of her discoveries. She struggles to gain funding and widespread acceptance for her theories.
*   Allies and Adversaries: Margaret finds a few key allies – perhaps a supportive mentor, a forward-thinking farmer, or a fellow female scientist – who believe in her work and help her navigate the patriarchal scientific community. She also faces direct opposition from those who cling to outdated methods or are unwilling to acknowledge a woman's genius.

Act III: The Harvest of Hope
*   The Solution Emerges: Margaret's perseverance pays off. She identifies rust-resistant wheat varieties or develops effective strategies for controlling the spread of the disease, offering a tangible solution to the crisis. This could involve breeding new strains or understanding the epidemiology to advise on planting practices.
*   Implementation and Impact: Despite lingering skepticism, her methods are eventually adopted on a wider scale. The next harvest season sees a dramatic reduction in crop losses, and the Canadian wheat fields begin to flourish once more, averting the impending famine and revitalizing the nation's economy.
*   Legacy and Recognition: Margaret Newton receives belated but significant recognition for her groundbreaking contributions. While she may not seek the limelight, her work fundamentally changes agricultural science and secures her place as an unsung hero who saved countless lives. The film ends with a sense of her quiet triumph and the enduring impact of her dedication on global food security.
