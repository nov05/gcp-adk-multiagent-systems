Logline: A dazzling Hollywood siren secretly harnesses her extraordinary intellect to co-invent a revolutionary communication system during World War II, battling skepticism and societal expectations to leave an indelible mark on the future of technology.

Three-Act Plot Outline:

Act 1: The Golden Cage and a Restless Mind
*   **Introduction to Hedy Lamarr:** We meet Hedy at the height of her Hollywood fame – beautiful, glamorous, and seemingly living the dream. However, beneath the surface, she is intellectually restless and frustrated by the superficiality of her public image. Flashbacks or subtle hints reveal her early life, perhaps her escape from an oppressive marriage and her arrival in America, driven by ambition and a desire for freedom.
*   **The Spark of Invention:** As World War II escalates, Hedy, an Austrian immigrant, feels a deep personal urgency to contribute to the Allied war effort. She sees the limitations of current military technology, particularly the vulnerability of torpedo guidance systems to jamming. Her scientific curiosity, often dismissed by those around her, begins to stir.
*   **Meeting George Antheil:** Hedy encounters avant-garde composer George Antheil, a fellow unconventional thinker. Their initial interactions are perhaps humorous or unexpected, but they quickly discover a shared passion for innovation and a mutual respect for each other's intellect, laying the groundwork for their collaboration.

Act 2: The Secret War and Unsung Genius
*   **The Birth of Frequency Hopping:** Hedy and George begin their clandestine work, using her knowledge of torpedoes and his understanding of synchronized piano rolls to develop the "Secret Communication System" – a frequency hopping technology designed to make torpedoes undetectable. This period is filled with late-night sessions, scientific breakthroughs, and the constant fear of discovery, all while Hedy maintains her demanding acting career.
*   **Battling Bureaucracy and Skepticism:** Despite their groundbreaking invention, Hedy and George face immense resistance from the military establishment. Their ideas are dismissed, partly due to Hedy's celebrity status and the unconventional nature of their approach. The patent is granted, but the technology is shelved, deemed too complex or simply overlooked.
*   **Personal Toll and Public Facade:** Hedy struggles with the emotional toll of her secret work and the lack of recognition for her intellectual contributions. She continues to shine on screen, but privately, she grapples with the frustration of her invention being ignored, feeling her true potential is unseen by the world.

Act 3: The Echo of Genius
*   **Post-War Life and Fading Stardom:** As the war ends, Hedy's invention remains dormant. Her acting career eventually wanes, and she faces personal challenges, including struggles with her public image and the pressures of aging in Hollywood. The world largely forgets her scientific endeavors, remembering her only as a beautiful film star.
*   **The Rediscovery:** Decades later, as technology advances, the principles of Hedy and George's patent are rediscovered and recognized for their visionary brilliance. Engineers working on secure wireless communication, Bluetooth, and Wi-Fi realize the foundational importance of their frequency hopping concept.
*   **Legacy Unveiled:** Hedy, perhaps in her later years or posthumously, finally receives the recognition she deserved. The film culminates with a powerful montage showing the widespread impact of her invention on modern life, from mobile phones to secure communications.
